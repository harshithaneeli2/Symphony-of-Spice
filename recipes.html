Popular Malay (West Coast)

Nasi Lemak

History: Originally an agrarian meal, farmers and fishermen ate this heavy carbohydrate dish for sustained energy over a long day. It is now the national dish of Malaysia.

View Cooking Instructions

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Basmati Rice
  • 1 cup Coconut Milk & 1 Pandan Leaf
  • For Sambal: Dried chilies, belacan (shrimp paste), onions, garlic, tamarind, sugar.
  • Garnish: Fried anchovies (ikan bilis), peanuts, cucumber, hard-boiled egg.

How to Cook:

  1. Wash rice and cook with coconut milk, water, and pandan leaf in a rice cooker.
  2. Blend sambal ingredients and sauté in oil until fragrant and oil separates. Add tamarind juice, sugar, and salt.
  3. Fry anchovies and peanuts until crunchy.
  4. Serve rice hot accompanied by sambal and garnishes.

Healthy/Popular Chinese (Hainan/Klang Valley)

Hainanese Chicken Rice

History: Brought over by immigrants from Hainan province in southern China. They adapted the dish locally, creating a defining staple of Malaysian-Chinese gastronomy.

View Cooking Instructions

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole free-range chicken
  • Ginger, garlic, spring onions
  • Jasmine rice (cooked in chicken fat/broth)
  • Ginger-garlic chili sauce

How to Cook:

  1. Poach the chicken slowly in barely simmering water with ginger and spring onions until just cooked. Plunge into ice water.
  2. Render chicken fat in a wok; fry garlic and uncooked rice, then transfer to a cooker using the poaching broth.
  3. Chop chicken and serve with fragrant rice, cucumber, and chili drizzle.
Healthy/Popular South Indian (Penang/KL)

Banana Leaf Rice

History: Introduced by South Indian immigrants who came to Malaya during British colonial rule. Eating on a banana leaf adds flavor and is highly hygienic and eco-friendly.

View Cooking Instructions

Ingredients:

  • White Rice
  • Assorted vegetables (Cabbage poriyal, snake gourd)
  • Dhal/Curry gravy (Sambar)
  • Papadam and pickles

How to Cook (Vegetable Poriyal):

  1. Heat coconut oil. Add mustard seeds until they pop.
  2. Add chopped onions, dried chilies, and curry leaves.
  3. Toss in finely chopped cabbage and a pinch of turmeric. Stir fry until tender but still crunchy.
  4. Finish with freshly grated coconut. Serve hot on a cleaned banana leaf.
Healthy Indonesian (Java/Johor)

Soto Ayam

History: A fragrant, yellow chicken soup originating from Java, Indonesia. It became a beloved, healthy staple in the southern Malaysian state of Johor.

View Cooking Instructions

Ingredients:

  • Shredded boiled chicken breast
  • Rice vermicelli (Bihun) or compressed rice (Nasi Impit)
  • Chicken broth infused with turmeric, lemongrass, and galangal
  • Bean sprouts, fried shallots, begedil (potato patty)

How to Cook:

  1. Blend shallots, garlic, turmeric, and ginger into a paste. Sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add chicken broth, bruised lemongrass, and simmer.
  3. In a bowl, assemble noodles/rice cakes, bean sprouts, and shredded chicken.
  4. Pour hot boiling soup over the bowl. Top with crispy shallots and a squeeze of lime.
